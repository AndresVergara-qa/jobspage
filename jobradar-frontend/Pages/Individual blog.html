How to Prepare for a QA Interview

JobRadar Team

Published Jan 15, 2026

Quality Assurance is more than just finding bugs; it's about ensuring value and maintaining the integrity of the product. As the demand for robust software grows, the bar for QA interviews continues to rise.

1. Mastering the Technical Fundamentals

Before walking into the interview, you must have a rock-solid understanding of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) and how testing fits into each phase. Interviewers frequently ask about the difference between verification and validation, or how you might handle a regression cycle under tight deadlines.

"The best QA engineers don't just find problems; they understand the business logic well enough to prevent them from occurring in the first place."

— Senior QA Lead at JobRadar

Common Technical Questions:

  • Explain the difference between Black Box and White Box testing.
  • What is the 'Severity' vs 'Priority' of a bug? Provide examples.
  • How do you perform API testing using tools like Postman?
  • Describe your experience with automation frameworks like Selenium or Playwright.

2. Behavioral and Soft Skills

QA is a collaborative role. You'll spend as much time talking to developers and product managers as you do writing test scripts. Expect questions like "How do you handle a developer who insists their bug is actually a feature?" This tests your diplomacy and communication skills.
